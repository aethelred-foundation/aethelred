Design Principles

Token release architecture principles

The CSV model emphasizes disciplined unlock pacing, ecosystem growth capital, and explicit reserve governance.

01

Controlled launch float

Launch float is published only after the canonical token source pack is approved and released.

02

Long tail rewards

PoUW rewards are distributed over 120 months to support durable validator incentives.

03

Contributor alignment

Core contributor allocation uses a cliff then linear release to align long-term execution.

04

Ecosystem funding discipline

Builder and ecosystem programs are release-gated and published only when approved through canonical token disclosures.

05

Risk buffers

Insurance and contingency reserves are isolated with explicit governance controls.

06

Counterparty gating

Liquidity and external commercial counterparties are not named publicly until agreements are executed and cleared for disclosure.

Institutional Settlement

Stablecoin infrastructure inside the token model

The tokenomics and whitepaper place stablecoin routing inside the economic control surface: relayers bond AETHEL, five governance keys control critical actions, and reserve plus circuit-breaker rules constrain issuer-authorized value movement.

Zero liquidity pools

Routing is designed around issuer-authorized channels instead of AMM bridge inventory.

Two settlement modes

CCTP V2 is used for Circle burn-and-mint routing and TEE Issuer Mint is used for non-CCTP assets.

500,000 AETHEL bond

Relayers must post a 500,000 AETHEL bond and can be slashed for relay misbehavior.

3-of-5 institutional control

Five keys govern critical actions, with a minimum 7-day action delay for the contract control path.

Mode Channel Primary Use

Approved launch disclosures

Publish float, pricing, and counterparties only after the relevant approvals are complete.

Mainnet and TGE communications

Use the final approved disclosures only when the technical, legal, and commercial states are actually ready to support them.
