Zero-Knowledge Proofs

Prove AI computation correctness without revealing sensitive data. ZK proofs are the cryptographic foundation enabling privacy-preserving verification on Aethelred.

What Are Zero-Knowledge Proofs?

A zero-knowledge proof is a cryptographic method that allows one party (the prover) to convince another party (the verifier) that a statement is true, without revealing any information beyond the validity of the statement itself.

In Aethelred, this means you can prove an AI model produced a specific output, that the output meets certain criteria, or that computation followed specific rules — all without exposing the model weights, input data, or proprietary algorithms.

Core Properties

The Three Pillars of ZK

Every zero-knowledge proof system must satisfy these fundamental properties.

✓

Completeness

If the statement is true, an honest prover can always convince an honest verifier. Valid computations will always generate valid proofs.

🛡️

Soundness

If the statement is false, no cheating prover can convince the verifier (except with negligible probability). Invalid computations cannot produce valid proofs.

🔒

Zero-Knowledge

The verifier learns nothing beyond the truth of the statement. Model weights, training data, and proprietary logic remain completely private.
